Just 3 steps to get you going

1. Subscribe

Our system is powered by Google, so you will need to log in using your Google details, or you will need to set up a Google account. This is free and relatively painless to do. You get the benefits of security, payment via Google, and potentially lots of other free facilities. We will use your Google ‘gmail’ address for account correspondence. During the process of subscription you can opt in or not to receive product update messages. We promise to respect your privacy - see our privacy policy for details.

All subscriptions are for one year and we guarantee service for that year. Basic Accounts give you 10 free Action Cards with a traffic limit of one hit per second per card (that’s 3,600 hits per hour per card). You can subscribe for more cards in blocks of 100.

Premium accounts give you unlimited traffic and are for using Action Cards as part of major advertising campaigns where site traffic may reach high levels.

Custom accounts allow you to deal with short peaks in traffic and are specified to suit your needs.

2. Create

For each of your Action Cards you choose the appropriate card type and enter the appropriate information. For most cards you don’t worry about layout as this is done for you. Any fields you leave blank are not shown when the user accesses your card.

For flexibility some card types have free text blocks and we even have ‘blank’ cards that are completely free text. In this case you will need to format it yourself. These fields have some basic formatting tools.

3. Print

Having created a card you are taken to a screen that has the unique QR code for that card and the public title you have entered. You can print out, copy and paste, or download the image to use on your printed media.

Please note that there is a minimum size of that will work and that this depends on the user’s camera, the lighting conditions, and the distance of the code from the user. If the user can get up close, such as with a business card or leaflet, then a minimum of 32mm across (one and a quarter inches in old money) is recommended. On an advertising board above street level it may have to be several metres across. A rule of thumb is:

  • Width = Distance/10 (or 8 if conditions are poor, 6 if very poor)

The code should be black and white and there should always be a small white border around the edge. If you change or distort the image it may not work reliably. The QR codes should work through glass and you can encapsulate them and make other treatments to protect them for outdoor use. Please though test your process to see that it does not prevent the camera reading the code.

You can also...

Edit

You can later edit your cards on-line, via any browser. Each Action Card has a unique and randomly generated URL which is permanent, so users will always see the latest information you have entered for that card.

Analyse

By including a Google analytics code in the appropriate field you will be able to see the number of hits on your cards and analyse where the traffic comes from and when. This free service is from Google and allows you to monitor advertising and sales campaigns. If you use a different card for different locations you can track which shop windows or posters are bringing you business.

Card descriptions

Q-Action Business Card

A business card that goes anywhere except out-of-date

The Q-Action Business Card is your company or personal contact details in an easily viewable, down-loadable, and more importantly, actionable form. Remember you can put your Action QR tag on the side of a van, on a poster, exhibition stand, label or leaflet - it’s not just restricted to your business card. You can also add a current marketing message to the card.

Not only can you be contacted quickly and easily, but you can update the details if you move. You can even change the details temporarily while you are on holiday, or if you are a company and your personnel change, then you can change the card to show to a new contact.

Q-Action Goto Card

Takes the user to the web page you choose

A Goto Card provides a jumping off point. The user never sees a ‘card’ as such. Instead, their browser is immediately redirected to the web page you specify in the card. The unique feature is that you can easily change the landing page that the user is taken to.

This is a great way to have different landing pages for different seasons, or for changing conditions. If you have a complex web site then rather than changing the offering on a fixed page you can simply change the page the user sees. Simple!

Q-Action Location Card

Information, directions, publicity, and up-to-the minute offers

A Location Card tells the user about nearby attractions and gives them action information such as maps, directions, phone numbers, and points of interest or things to do.

They can be used for promoting local services, such as the cafe just round the corner, with the added twist that you can promote specific offers that you can change as often as you want. Remember that you can put multiple copies of the same tag at locations around you, or you can have several tags for different offers.

Q-Action Offer Card

A ‘buy now’ facility for ‘window shoppers’ and out of hours sales

Great for shop windows and display cabinets. The card contains details of the specific product and offer, plus a ‘buy now’ button to take the user to your web site or e-commerce facility or a phone number. As Q-Action codes work through glass you can be selling when you are closed, or generating business from remote display locations. If the offer changes, there is no need to update the displays - you simply update your offer card on-line. They can also be put in car windows as part of a ‘for sale’ notice. Travel agents and estate agents can also use them in window displays to allow window shoppers to take away offer details at any time.

Q-Action Membership Card

Everlasting membership cards that show the current status

Organisations need not issue new membership cards each year. The Action Membership Card is for ever and records on-line the key details such as the current membership status associated with each card.

By having an individual Q-Action Membership Card for each member, not only can officials scan the cards to check validity and ownership, but members can scan their own card to check that their details are correct.

Q-Action Service Card

Product or installation cards that record and update information

Service, warranty, user instructions and other information attached to a product or an installation can be used over a long time, but can easily become out of date. Action Service Cards can be updated either centrally or on location in order to keep on-line records of visits and due dates that are accessible by scanning a label on the device. As well as informing owners of operating instructions, warranty periods etc., in the event that the user scans the code due to a problem it can even alert them to product safety recall information or to known issues that have recently arisen.

Q-Action Guided tour Card

Bringing places alive with knowledge and information

Guided tour cards enable you to provide comprehensive visitor information at a set of locations. Each card can relate to a specific location and is easily updated as visitor information changes, including features and locations that are temporarily closed.

Note: Q-Action Guided Tour Cards are text only. For sophisticated rich-media audio-visual guides we offer another service Q-Point, which also works from QR code tags.

Q-Action ICE (In Case of Emergency) Card

Emergency advice, contact information, and nearest help

ICE Cards can be used on vehicles, buildings, or at remote locations. They contain up-to-date information on what to do in an emergency. This can include ‘one click’ emergency phone numbers, nearby help points, location information to direct helpers, or location-specific advice to people who are lost or stuck.

Note: In case mobile reception or wi-fi is not available at the intended location, you have the option to produce a code tag that is self-contained and so will read the details directly into the users’ phone. Unlike the on-line facility, this self-contained tag will need to be re-printed if you edit the card details.

Q-Action Rota Card

Rota lists that always show who is on duty today

This clever card is created by uploading your duty rotas, comprising people, roles, or just things, in a structured format with date information. Any user scanning the tag sees who is on duty, or what is happening today, along with any other information you specify, such as contact details, or current notices.

This is a great way to put regularly changing information at outdoor locations where updating notice boards is time-consuming or impractical. You upload the advance rota from anywhere with an Internet connection, including your phone, and it then runs for months on its own. Users always scan the same tag, which can be on a notice-board, a gateway, a building, but each time they see, and can take with them, today’s rota. Neat uh?

Q-Action Stock Card

Stock control cards that you can update centrally or on location

Stock cards provide an electronic way to update stock information either centrally from a head office or on the ground at the location of the stock. They provide the basis of a simple stock control and information system where any user with access to the tag can read the stock card details.

Because tags can be encapsulated and even read from behind glass or at a distance, they are suitable for environments where ‘card’ cards and hand writing would not be practical.

Q-Action Event Card

Up to the minute programmes for events and shows

How many show programmes can be updated during the day of the show? This one can. Event cards list the time, location, and brief details of events on a programme. They are accessed via a fixed tag that is printed on show literature or distributed on site posters.

The programme can be updated minute-by-minute as factors like weather and availability cause adjustments. If a demonstration is now full, or the Red Arrows have been delayed, then you can let thousands of spectators have that information in an instant.

Even after the event the Q-Action code can be re-used and point to a suitable page on your website, perhaps advertising the next event.
